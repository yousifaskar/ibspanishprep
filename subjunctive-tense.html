The Subjunctive Mood

What is a grammatical "mood"?

A mood in grammar refers to a verb category/form in which speakers use to express their attitude toward a certain type of situation. For example, we've been learning the indicative mood, which is used to express statements of fact and knowledge.

  • Es mi casa.
  • A ella le gusta la comida.
  • Mis amigos fueron a mi casa ayer
The subjunctive mood focuses on the concept of possibilty and desire. The speaker decides to create a new possibility in the world or desires something from a situation. For example, in English:
  • I want him to buy me a car.
  • John, it's possible that I have to work from home today.
  • I am going to the store so that we could have a good party.

There are three different subjunctive tenses: the present subjunctive, the past subjunctive, and the past perfect subjunctive. This page will focus on the first two.

Conjugating Regularly in the Present Subjunctive

The Process:

  1. Conjugate the verb in the first form of the present
  2. Remove the 'o'
  3. Add the following endings (also known as opposite endings)

ER/IR endings

Some Examples...

  • Tener:
    • First form of present - tengo
    • Remove the 'o' - teng
    • Add opposite endings: tenga, tengas, tenga, tengamos, tengáis, tengan
  • Decir:
    • First form of present - digo
    • Remove the 'o' - dig
    • Add opposite endings: diga, digas, diga, digaamos, digáis, digan
